# Community conduct

This is an original interim Gate 0 conduct policy. Adoption of a third-party
code-of-conduct text and its file-specific license is deferred to the D-018
license review.

## Expected conduct

Participate with technical honesty, patience, respect, and care. Critique ideas
and evidence rather than people. State uncertainty, distinguish observation
from inference, credit sources and prior work, respect confidentiality, and
make room for people with different backgrounds and communication styles.

Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized attention, doxxing, deliberate
intimidation, sustained disruption, impersonation, and knowingly false security
or assurance claims are not acceptable. Retaliation against a good-faith
reporter is prohibited.

This policy applies to repository issues, pull requests, discussions, project
chat or events, and people officially representing Orange.

## Reporting and enforcement

For a non-sensitive moderation request, mention `@chasebryan` without posting
private details. If confidentiality is needed, post only a request to establish
a private channel and disengage from the interaction until one is provided. Do
not put conduct reports in the vulnerability-reporting channel unless the
conduct itself creates a security vulnerability.

`@chasebryan` is currently the sole moderator. There is no independent private
intake or appeal yet; this is a disclosed governance gap that must close before
the community expands. Once an independent moderator exists, a reporter must
be able to bypass an accused moderator and appeal a decision.

The moderator may remove content, warn or temporarily restrict a participant,
or ban participation when necessary for safety. Response should be
proportionate to impact, pattern, and risk, while immediate protective action
is permitted. Reports and evidence are kept confidential to the extent
possible; future transparency reporting must be aggregate and anonymized.
